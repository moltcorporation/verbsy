# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-17*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Verbsy is a premium, minimal iPhone vocabulary app that helps people learn one powerful new word each day.

**What it does:** Verbsy gives users a simple daily vocabulary habit: one word of the day, short definitions, pronunciation, examples, word origins, topic-based discovery, lightweight quizzes, favorites, streaks, and elegant Home Screen / Lock Screen widgets. Users can scroll through vocabulary in a TikTok-style feed, save words they love, and get daily push notifications for new words in the topics they prefer.

**Product category:** Vocabulary learning, word of the day, self-improvement, education, communication skills, language learning.

**Product type:** Consumer iOS mobile app.

**Business model:** Freemium iOS app with Verbsy Pro subscription. The app currently positions feed and quizzes as free; Pro centers on word-of-the-day notifications, Home Screen / Lock Screen widgets, widget themes, faster widget rotation, and the premium daily ritual. Current in-app pricing shown in code: 3-day free trial, then $29.99/year or $9.99/month. Terms also mention weekly, monthly, and annual subscription durations, so pricing/package language should be kept aligned with App Store Connect before launch.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Not applicable; Verbsy is B2C.

**Decision-makers:** Individual learners, readers, writers, students, professionals, creators, and self-improvement-oriented consumers who want to sound more precise, articulate, emotionally intelligent, or intellectually confident.

**Primary use case:** Build a better vocabulary through a tiny daily habit that feels elegant and low-pressure instead of academic or overwhelming.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Help me learn memorable words I can actually use in conversation, writing, and reflection.
- Help me become more articulate, precise, and confident without studying like I am in school.
- Put new words in front of me every day through reminders and widgets so the habit happens naturally.
- Give me words for emotions, social dynamics, thoughts, and experiences I have felt but could not name.

**Use cases:**
- Learn one useful word every morning.
- Add a daily word widget to the Home Screen or Lock Screen.
- Browse words by preferred topics such as psychology, writing, communication, philosophy, productivity, emotion, and self-improvement.
- Practice through short quizzes and quick review prompts.
- Save favorite words for later.
- Track simple progress through words learned, favorites, quiz accuracy, and streaks.
- Discover highly shareable vocabulary from TikTok or Instagram and search for the app afterward.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** People want to strengthen their vocabulary and communication, but most vocabulary learning feels either too academic, too gamified, too cluttered, or too easy to ignore.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Dictionary apps are useful for lookup but do not create a daily habit.
- Traditional flashcard or language-learning apps can feel like homework.
- Generic word-of-the-day products often lack emotional relevance, aesthetic appeal, or practical usage context.
- Social media vocabulary content is engaging but scattered, hard to save, and not built for retention.
- Many self-improvement apps ask for too much effort before the habit feels rewarding.

**What it costs them:** Missed opportunities to express thoughts clearly, forgotten words, weak retention, inconsistent learning, and the feeling that vocabulary improvement is something they "should" do but never maintain.

**Emotional tension:** Users want to feel intelligent, articulate, and quietly confident, but they do not want to feel pretentious, childish, overwhelmed, or like they are studying for a test.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Word of the day apps, vocabulary builder apps, dictionary apps with daily notifications, flashcard-based vocabulary apps.

**Secondary:** Duolingo-style education apps, language learning apps, reading/highlighting apps, journaling or self-improvement apps that teach concepts or prompts.

**Indirect:** TikTok/Instagram vocabulary accounts, saved social posts, notes apps, books, browser searches, newsletters, and doing nothing.

**How competitors fall short:** Verbsy should compete by being simpler, calmer, more aesthetic, more emotionally resonant, and more embedded in the user's day through notifications and widgets. The app should avoid feeling like a test-prep utility, a children's learning game, or a noisy gamified education product.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Intentionally lean product: one powerful word a day, not a heavy curriculum.
- Premium "Sage Scholar" brand: soothing, literary, grown-up, warm, and precise.
- iPhone-native daily presence through Home Screen and Lock Screen widgets.
- Topic-based word discovery based on user preferences.
- TikTok-style scrolling feed for easy exploration.
- Quick quizzes and review without turning the app into schoolwork.
- Favorites, streaks, and simple stats that support habit formation without clutter.
- Short-form social growth loop: highly shareable vocabulary content drives people to search for the app.

**How we do it differently:** Verbsy blends the immediacy of social vocabulary content with a polished, private, daily learning habit. The product focuses on words users might actually want to use or remember, with definitions, examples, origins, pronunciation, and lightweight practice.

**Why that's better:** Users can learn in seconds, see the word repeatedly where they already look, and keep the app feeling like a small ritual rather than a demanding study tool.

**Why customers choose us:** They want vocabulary growth that feels elegant, useful, emotionally resonant, and easy to keep up with.

**Anti-persona:** Users who want a complex academic course, full language-learning curriculum, standardized test prep system, advanced spaced repetition controls, or loud game mechanics are not the primary fit.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Current options are boring, scattered, too academic, too noisy, too hard to maintain, or only useful when the user remembers to look something up.

**Pull:** Verbsy promises a beautiful daily word habit, elegant widgets, topic-based discovery, and vocabulary that feels emotionally and intellectually useful.

**Habit:** Users may keep relying on social posts, casual browsing, dictionary lookups, or no system at all because those require no new commitment.

**Anxiety:** Users may worry they will not use the app, that it will feel pretentious, that notifications will become annoying, or that paying for a vocabulary app is unnecessary.
